# Before You Read

This repository is a personal review project built from concepts studied online and reorganised into concise Markdown notes. It is not a formal course, not an official Google Cloud resource, and not a substitute for primary documentation. The goal is modest: to collect, simplify, and structure what was learned during study, then make that recap readable enough that it may also help someone else.

These notes are intentionally selective and explanatory. They aim to make complex concepts easier to revisit, especially where product naming, platform boundaries, or AI terminology can become confusing. In some places, examples may be simplified or invented purely for illustration. That is done to improve clarity, not to claim completeness or operational precision.

## What this repository is

- A structured recap of concepts related to Google Cloud GenAI Leader topics.
- A personal study aid converted into public-facing Markdown notes.
- A practical reference for readers who want compact conceptual explanations.
- A lightweight knowledge base rather than a full academic or technical manual.

## What this repository is not

- It is not an official training course.
- It is not certification guidance issued by Google.
- It is not a lab manual or implementation handbook.
- It is not guaranteed to be complete, current, or sufficient for exam preparation by itself.

## How these notes were built

The material in this repository comes from personal study of publicly available online learning content, including transcript-based review and conceptual reorganisation. The text should therefore be read as interpretive study notes rather than primary authority. Product interfaces, naming conventions, model availability, and platform workflows can change over time, sometimes quickly.

## How to read these notes well

- Treat them as a secondary layer of explanation, not as the final source of truth.
- Use them to understand concepts, distinctions, and product logic.
- Verify important technical details against official documentation when needed.
- Assume that some examples are simplified to illustrate mechanisms more clearly.
- Remember that cloud interfaces and product packaging may evolve after these notes were written.

## Practical warning

Some of the concepts covered in this repository belong to fast-moving product categories: AI tooling, agent systems, cloud search, model hosting, and enterprise integrations. In these areas, small wording differences can matter, and product behaviour may differ across editions, permissions, locations, and account types. For that reason, these notes should be read as a review companion, not as an operational specification.

## Recommended reading order

1. Read this file first.
2. Read `disclaimer-and-limits.md` next.
3. Then move to the technical notes, using them as conceptual summaries rather than formal documentation.

## Final note

The spirit of this repository is simple. These notes are only the result of things learned while studying online. They have been reorganised here as a review and shared in case they may help someone else do the same.
